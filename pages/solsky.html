Writing prompt — this is a two-layer problem: Layer 1 is the market problem (Indian pet care is reactive, not preventive — global brands formulated for Western dogs, high vet dependency, treat culture vs. nutrition). Key Decisions

⚡ Homepage: lead with the problem or lead with the product?
Options considered
  • Product-first: hero shows product range, shop CTA above fold
  • Problem-first: lead with "Indian pet care is reactive" before products
  • Lifestyle-first: dogs, emotional imagery, brand feeling
What we chose
  • Problem-first narrative structure
  • Positioned prevention before products
  • Products introduced within the system context
Why: A new brand in a low-trust category can't lead with product — there's no equity yet. Leading with a sharp problem statement ("most pet products show up after something's already wrong") creates category tension before the brand enters. By the time a product appears, the user understands why prevention matters. The product becomes the answer to a question already in their head.
