Forcing a visitor to self-identify before seeing anything signals the brand doesn't know how to speak to everyone. A seed-stage brand asking "who are you?" at the door reads as uncertain, not personalised.

Three front doors fragment an already early-stage brand into three weaker versions of itself. No single coherent brand narrative ever emerges. This direction was abandoned.
Problem: A customer researching Praan for investment who also happens to operate factories would never see the operator content. Siloed tracks prevent the brand from speaking with a single, confident voice.
