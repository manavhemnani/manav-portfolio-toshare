Percept:
Learn Photography

Percept is a guided learning platform designed to help beginners understand photography through visual explanations rather than technical jargon. The system breaks down camera concepts into interactive modules that simulate how exposure, aperture, and lighting affect real images.

Where It Started

The origin

During design school I noticed that many of us had slowly drifted away from the hobbies that once sparked our curiosity. Photography had been one of mine — something I had practiced for years through Nikon courses and personal experimentation. When I tried returning to it, most learning resources felt overly technical and disconnected from the joy of exploring the craft.

That experience revealed a broader gap: most photography platforms teach camera settings, but very few teach people how to see.

—04

Constraints

✏️
Writing prompt: Learning app constraints are different from utility app constraints. Think about: attention span during a lesson, the transition from in-app to real-world context, the fact that progress in photography is hard to measure objectively, and the risk that gamification mechanics undermine the meditative quality of actually shooting. Document the constraints that shaped your design, not generic ones.
Learning requires doing — passive content doesn't build muscle memory Progress in photography is subjective — hard to gamify meaningfully Lesson context switches: in-app → real world → back in-app Risk: gamification mechanics undermine the meditative quality of shooting Users will open this app outdoors — legibility in sunlight [ Add your actual constraints ]

[ Pick the 1–2 most interesting constraints and explain how they directly shaped a design decision. The "learning requires doing" constraint is the core one — how did this force a structural decision about how lessons are designed? ]
