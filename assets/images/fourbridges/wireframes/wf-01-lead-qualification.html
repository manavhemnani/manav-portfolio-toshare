Why the checklist fails A "We work best with…" criteria list frames the section as a filter — something the firm imposes. It positions selectiveness as an operational constraint, not a strategic choice. The CTA "Contact Us" has no qualifier: anyone reads it and wonders if they should. High-quality prospects are most sensitive to wasted meetings.
Why front-facing selectiveness works Naming a section "We Are Selective" makes the filter explicit and prestigious — like a club that admits not everyone. The "Assess Your Fit" CTA routes high-intent users to a structured intake form. Founders who pass feel pre-qualified; the firm wastes zero discovery calls on misaligned mandates. The section functions as a psychological pre-qualifier, not a barrier.
How this translated to final UI
Section named "We Are Selective" placed prominently before the Engage CTA in homepage scroll
Criteria presented as positive declarations, not exclusionary checkboxes
"Assess Your Fit" button routes to structured intake form — not a generic contact page
Placement before the CTA ensures only filtered, confident prospects click Engage
What this decision improves
Credibility: Selectiveness signals quality of client relationship, not scale
Conversion: Higher-intent contacts arrive at first call pre-qualified
Clarity: Visitor knows immediately whether they're the right fit
Trust: Founders feel vetted, not rejected — engagement increases
