About

Product designer.
Industrial thinker.

I'm Manav — trained in industrial design, now designing digital products for the physical world. I've designed companion apps for hardware ecosystems, spatial training experiences in VR, and mobile tools for everyday learning.

My industrial design background gives me a different starting point: I think about systems before screens, constraints before concepts, and physical behavior before digital interaction.

Outside the work: travel photographer, DJ, tennis player. I'm drawn to systems — whether that's a mixing board, a tennis rally, or an onboarding flow.

Full Story →
01

Constraints first

I start every project by mapping what I can't do. Constraints reveal design opportunity faster than open-ended exploration.

02

Legibility under pressure

The best interfaces work when users are distracted, anxious, or in a hurry. I design for the worst moment, not the ideal one.

03

Physical logic in digital space

When digital interactions borrow from physical behavior — weight, resistance, momentum — users trust them faster.

04

Ship to learn

Concepts teach craft. Shipped products teach humility. I pursue both, equally.
